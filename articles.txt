Sharkfunded’s help center provides detailed explanations of how these metrics are calculated to ensure traders can manage their risk effectively.Balance-Based Daily DrawdownSharkfunded utilizes a Daily Loss Limit based on the starting balance of the day. The "day" typically resets at 5:00 PM EST (Server Time). This means that if a trader starts the day with $100,000 and the daily limit is 5%, their account will be breached if the equity or balance drops below $95,000 during that 24-hour period.The formula is expressed as:$$Limit_{daily} = Balance_{start} - (Balance_{start} \times 0.05)$$A critical nuance is that the daily limit is based on balance, not equity. If a trader has open positions that are in profit, that profit does not increase their daily loss allowance for the following day until the positions are closed and the balance is updated. Conversely, if a trader has floating losses at the time of the daily reset, those losses are factored into the starting balance for the new day, effectively tightening the limit for the next session.Static vs. Trailing DrawdownSharkfunded's use of a "Static" maximum total drawdown is a significant advantage for the trader. In a trailing drawdown model, the floor moves up as the trader's equity increases, which can "trap" a trader's profits and make it difficult to manage larger positions. In Sharkfunded’s static model, the floor remains fixed at the initial percentage of the starting balance.For example, on a $100,000 account with a 10% static drawdown, the floor is $90,000. If the trader grows the account to $110,000, they now have a $20,000 buffer before they breach the account ($110,000 - $90,000). This provides a "safety cushion" that rewards profitable traders and allows them to withstand occasional periods of market volatility without losing their funded status.Trading Rules and Market ConductTo maintain the integrity of its capital and its relationship with liquidity providers, Sharkfunded enforces a set of rules regarding trading styles and behaviors.
